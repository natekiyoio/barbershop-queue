BACKGROUND

A. The Client is of the opinion that the Contractor has the necessary qualifications, experience, and abilities to provide marketing strategy, execution, brand management, and client success services to the Client.

B. The Contractor is agreeable to providing such services to the Client on the terms and conditions set out in this Agreement.

IN CONSIDERATION OF the matters described above and of the mutual benefits and obligations set forth in this Agreement, the receipt and sufficiency of which consideration is hereby acknowledged, the Client and the Contractor (individually the "Party" and collectively the "Parties") agree as follows:

1. SERVICES PROVIDED

1.1 The Client hereby agrees to engage the Contractor to provide the Client with the following services (the "Services"):

1.1.1 Marketing Strategy & Execution:

  • Execute marketing initiatives across social media, content, and outbound channels
  • Manage brand voice, positioning, and consistency across all touchpoints
  • Collaborate with founders on campaigns and growth experiments
  • Develop and implement content marketing strategies
  • Oversee paid and organic marketing channel performance
  • Create and maintain marketing calendars and campaign schedules

1.1.2 Client Success & Relationship Management:

  • Act as the primary point of contact for all active clients after onboarding
  • Handle client questions, concerns, and day-to-day communication
  • Determine when issues can be resolved independently vs. escalated to the founders
  • Maintain client satisfaction and retention
  • Identify upsell and cross-sell opportunities
  • Manage client onboarding processes and ensure smooth transitions

1.1.3 Retention & Feedback Loop:

  • Ensure client satisfaction, retention, and identify upsell opportunities
  • Collect feedback and relay insights to operations and systems teams
  • Monitor client health scores and engagement metrics
  • Develop and implement retention strategies
  • Create customer success playbooks and documentation

1.1.4 Brand Presence & Community Building:

  • Manage social media presence and engagement
  • Build and nurture online community
  • Develop brand partnerships and collaborations
  • Oversee public relations and media outreach

1.2 The Services will also include any other tasks which the Parties may agree on. The Contractor hereby agrees to provide such Services to the Client.

2. TERM OF AGREEMENT

2.1 The term of this Agreement (the "Term") will begin on the date of this Agreement and will remain in effect until completion of the Services, subject to earlier termination as provided in this Agreement.

2.2 In the event that either Party wishes to terminate this Agreement, that Party will be required to provide 30 days' written notice to the other Party.

2.3 In the event that either Party breaches a material provision under this Agreement, the non-defaulting Party may terminate this Agreement immediately and pursue all available legal remedies.

2.4 This Agreement may also be terminated at any time by mutual written agreement of the Parties.

2.5 Upon termination of this Agreement:

  • All pending compensation for completed work shall be paid within 30 days
  • The Contractor shall return all Client materials, data, and property
  • The Contractor shall provide a comprehensive transition document including active campaigns, client statuses, and ongoing initiatives
  • The Contractor shall transfer all social media accounts, passwords, and access credentials
  • The Contractor shall complete any client handoff documentation
3. PERFORMANCE

3.1 The Contractor agrees to perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner and to meet the performance metrics outlined in Exhibit A.

3.2 The Contractor will endeavor to meet agreed-upon key performance indicators (KPIs), which may include but are not limited to:

  • Marketing campaign performance metrics (reach, engagement, conversions)
  • Client satisfaction scores (NPS, CSAT, or similar)
  • Client retention rate and churn reduction
  • Client response time and resolution time
  • Content production volume and quality standards
  • Social media growth and engagement rates
  • Revenue impact from upsells and renewals

3.3 Both Parties agree to regular performance reviews to discuss metrics, provide feedback, and make adjustments as necessary.

4. COMPENSATION

4.1 All compensation under this Agreement shall be paid in Canadian Dollars (CAD).

4.2 The Contractor will be compensated at a rate of 20% of Net Collected Revenue for the month, paid monthly. Compensation is earned only upon the Client's actual receipt of cleared funds from customers.

4.3 Definitions: For the purposes of this Agreement, "Net Collected Revenue" means the total cash collected by the Client from customers attributable to marketing and client success activities, excluding:

  • Sales taxes (GST/HST/PST/QST), value-added taxes, or other government levies
  • Refunds, returns, credits, or chargebacks
  • Third-party platform fees (e.g., Stripe/merchant processing fees)
  • Shipping, handling, and insurance costs
  • Bad debt or uncollectible receivables

4.4 The Contractor will be entitled to the reimbursement of the following expenses upon providing valid receipts:

  • Pre-approved marketing software/tools and subscriptions (e.g., social media management, analytics, design tools)
  • Pre-approved advertising spend and campaign costs
  • Pre-approved professional development and training related to Services
  • Pre-approved travel expenses related to client meetings or marketing events
  • Other expenses as mutually agreed upon in writing

4.5 Invoices submitted by the Contractor will be due within days of receipt. Revenue-based compensation shall be calculated and paid by the 15th day of the following month.

4.6 The Contractor will be responsible for all income taxes, self-employment taxes, and other applicable taxes on compensation received under this Agreement.

4.7 Clawback & Set-Off: In the event that a customer receives a refund or initiates a chargeback for revenue upon which the Contractor has already been paid a commission, the Client shall be entitled to "claw back" the overpayment. The Client may, at its discretion, deduct such amounts from any future payments due to the Contractor ("Set-Off").

4.8 Audit Rights: The Contractor shall have the right, no more than once per calendar year and at their own expense, to appoint a neutral third-party CPA to audit the Client's relevant financial records to verify the accuracy of Net Collected Revenue calculations. Such audit requires 14 days' written notice and must be conducted during normal business hours.

6. CONFIDENTIALITY

6.1 Confidential information ("Confidential Information") refers to any data or information relating to the Client, whether business or personal, which would reasonably be considered to be private or proprietary to the Client including, but not limited to:

  • Customer lists, client databases, and contact information
  • Marketing strategies, campaigns, and creative materials
  • Brand guidelines, positioning strategies, and market research
  • Pricing structures, profit margins, and financial information
  • Business strategies, product roadmaps, and growth plans
  • Client communications, feedback, and relationship history
  • Proprietary tools, templates, and processes
  • Social media strategies and analytics data

6.2 The Contractor agrees not to disclose, divulge, reveal, report, or use for any purpose any Confidential Information which the Contractor has obtained, except as authorized by the Client or as required by law.

6.3 All written and oral information and materials disclosed or provided by the Client to the Contractor under this Agreement is Confidential Information regardless of whether it was provided before or after the date of this Agreement.

6.4 This confidentiality obligation shall survive the termination of this Agreement indefinitely.

7. NON-SOLICITATION AND NON-COMPETE

7.1 During the term of this Agreement and for a period of months thereafter, the Contractor agrees not to:

  • Directly or indirectly solicit, contact, or do business with any of the Client's customers or clients that the Contractor had contact with during the term of this Agreement
  • Solicit or recruit any of the Client's employees or other contractors
  • Provide similar marketing or client success services to direct competitors of the Client in the same market segment (if agreed upon)
  • Use any client lists, contact information, or relationship insights for personal benefit or for another business

7.2 The Contractor acknowledges that any breach of this section would cause irreparable harm to the Client and that monetary damages would be inadequate.

9.2 The Contractor will be solely responsible for:

  • Determining the method, details, and means of performing the Services
  • Providing their own equipment, tools, and workspace unless otherwise agreed
  • Setting their own work schedule within any agreed-upon availability requirements for client communications
  • All taxes, insurance, and benefits

9.3 Neither Party has authority to enter into contracts or agreements on behalf of the other Party without written consent.

9.4 The Contractor acknowledges that they may be required to maintain regular business hours or specific availability windows for client communication purposes, but this does not alter their independent contractor status.

10.1 Except as otherwise provided in this Agreement, the Contractor may, at the Contractor's absolute discretion, engage a third party subcontractor to perform some or all of the obligations of the Contractor under this Agreement provided that:

  • The Client provides prior written approval of any subcontractor

  • The Contractor remains responsible for all work performed by the subcontractor
  • For client-facing work, subcontractors must be introduced to and approved by affected clients

  • Marketing platforms and tools necessary to perform the Services
  • Brand assets, guidelines, and approved materials
  • Client information and CRM systems
  • Internal communication channels and documentation
  • Necessary training on company products, services, and processes

11.2 The Contractor is responsible for maintaining the security and confidentiality of all access credentials and must immediately report any suspected security breaches.

11.3 Upon termination, the Contractor must return or destroy all Client property and revoke all access permissions.

12. SOCIAL MEDIA AND BRAND REPRESENTATION

12.1 The Contractor acknowledges that they may represent the Client's brand publicly through social media, community engagement, and client communications.

12.2 All public-facing communications on behalf of the Client must:

  • Adhere to the Client's brand guidelines and voice
  • Comply with all applicable laws and regulations
  • Obtain prior approval for major announcements or potentially sensitive content
  • Maintain professional standards and ethical practices

12.3 The Contractor must not make any statements or representations that could bind the Client legally or financially without express written authorization.

12.4 All social media accounts, domain names, and platforms created for or used on behalf of the Client remain the absolute and exclusive property of the Client.

12.5 Security & Access Handover: The Contractor must maintain Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all Client-related accounts where available. Upon termination of this Agreement for any reason, the Contractor shall immediately provide a comprehensive list of all access credentials and perform a structured "handover" of all accounts to the Client before final compensation is released.

13. CLIENT DATA PROTECTION

13.1 The Contractor acknowledges that they will have access to sensitive client data and personal information in the course of providing client success services.

13.2 The Contractor agrees to:

  • Comply with all applicable privacy laws and regulations including PIPEDA (Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act)
  • Use client data only for the purposes of providing the Services
  • Implement reasonable security measures to protect client data
  • Immediately report any data breaches or security incidents
  • Not store client data on personal devices or unsecured systems without authorization

13.3 The Contractor shall indemnify the Client for any damages resulting from the Contractor's breach of data protection obligations.
